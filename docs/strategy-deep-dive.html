2. Strategy 1: Momentum Breakout

Core Logic

Buy options in the direction of the current trend when a high-scoring, liquid option is found with sufficient volume. The trend must be confirmed by 4+ technical indicators, but NOT fully exhausted (capped at 92% strength). Entry is only taken if the last 3 price candles are actually moving in the trend direction (momentum confirmation).

When It Triggers

IF trend_direction == BULLISH and 60% ≤ strength < 92%: Buy CE (Call) near ATM IF trend_direction == BEARISH and 55% ≤ bear_agreement < 92%: Buy PE (Put) near ATM REQUIREMENTS: - Volume > 10,000 contracts - Score ≥ 50/100 - Premium between 50 and 500 (BANKNIFTY max 500) - RSI not overbought (CE: <75 NIFTY, <68 BANKNIFTY) - RSI not oversold (PE: >20) - Last 3 candles moving in trend direction

Stop Loss Calculation

SL = Entry - (ATR × multiplier × estimated_delta) Where: ATR = 14-period Average True Range of spot multiplier = 1.5 (NIFTY) or 3.0 (BANKNIFTY) estimated_delta = 0.4 (slightly OTM approximation) FLOOR: SL never tighter than min_sl_pct of premium NIFTY: min 10% of premium BANKNIFTY: min 15% of premium CAP: SL never wider than 50% of premium

Target Calculation

risk = Entry - SL Target = Entry + (risk × R:R ratio) R:R ratio: NIFTY: 2.0 (need 2x the risk as reward) BANKNIFTY: 1.5 (faster profit taking on volatile instrument)

Position Sizing

max_units = MAX_LOSS_PER_TRADE / risk_per_unit lots = floor(max_units / lot_size) lots = max(lots, 1) # minimum 1 lot quantity = lots × lot_size MAX_LOSS_PER_TRADE = Rs.2,000

3. Strategy 2: OI Reversal

Core Logic

When massive Open Interest builds up at a strike, option writers are defending that level. High Put OI at a strike means writers sold puts there and will defend it (support). High Call OI means resistance. This strategy buys options at levels where OI confirms the trend direction — it's essentially a "smart money is on your side" signal.

When It Triggers

BULLISH TREND + High Put OI (>200K) at nearby strike: Writers sold PUTs = defending that level as support → Buy CE near that strike (bullish confirmation) BEARISH TREND + High Call OI (>200K) at nearby strike: Writers sold CALLs = defending that level as resistance → Buy PE near that strike (bearish confirmation) Same SL/target/sizing rules as Momentum strategy

Why OI Works

High Put OI = Support
Writers sold puts at this strike. If price falls to this level, their puts become risky. They'll buy futures to hedge, pushing price UP. This creates a floor.
High Call OI = Resistance
Writers sold calls at this strike. If price rises to this level, their calls become risky. They'll sell futures to hedge, pushing price DOWN. This creates a ceiling.
OI Buildup = Conviction
Rising OI means NEW positions being created (not just rollovers). This shows fresh money betting on that level holding. The higher the OI, the stronger the level.

4. The 6 Indicators — Trend Detection Engine

The trend detector runs 6 independent indicators and aggregates their votes. Each indicator says BULL, BEAR, or NEUTRAL. The percentage of bullish votes determines the trend direction and strength.

Indicator 1
EMA Crossover (5/13)
What: Exponential Moving Average of last 5 candles vs last 13 candles.
Signal: EMA 5 > EMA 13 = BULL (short-term momentum is up). EMA 5 < EMA 13 = BEAR.
Why 5/13: Faster than traditional 9/21. Catches trend reversals earlier, especially important for BANKNIFTY's short intraday trends.
Weakness: Whipsaws in sideways markets. That's why we need 4+ indicators to agree.
Indicator 2
Price vs EMA 20
What: Is current price above or below the 20-period EMA?
Signal: Price > EMA 20 = BULL (price is above its recent average). Price < EMA 20 = BEAR.
Why 20: Shorter than traditional EMA 50 to detect bearish trends faster. EMA 50 was too slow — by the time it flagged bear, RSI was already at 15 and the move was over.
Strength: Simple, reliable, rarely gives false signals on strong trends.
Indicator 3
RSI (14-period)
What: Relative Strength Index — measures speed and magnitude of price changes (0-100 scale).
Signal: RSI > 55 = BULL. RSI < 45 = BEAR. 45-55 = NEUTRAL.
Why 55/45: Wider than traditional 60/40 so RSI contributes direction more often. The old 60/40 made RSI say NEUTRAL too much, weakening bear detection.
Also used as: Entry guard — blocks CE when RSI > 75 (NIFTY) / 68 (BANKNIFTY) to avoid buying into exhausted trends.
Indicator 4
MACD (12/26/9)
What: Moving Average Convergence Divergence — difference between fast and slow EMAs, with a signal line.
Signal: MACD histogram > 0 = BULL (fast EMA pulling away from slow = accelerating momentum). Histogram < 0 = BEAR.
Strength: Excellent at detecting momentum shifts. When histogram flips from negative to positive, it's often the START of a move, not the end.
Displayed in dashboard: Green/red histogram bars + MACD/signal lines.
Indicator 5
Supertrend (7, 2.5)
What: Volatility-based trend following indicator. Plots a line above (bearish) or below (bullish) price based on ATR.
Signal: Price above Supertrend line = BULL. Price below = BEAR. The line flips when price crosses it.
Why period=7, mult=2.5: Faster than default (10, 3.0) to catch reversals. The lower multiplier makes it more sensitive to trend changes.
Displayed in dashboard: Dashed green/red line on the spot chart.
Indicator 6
Price Action (3-candle slope)
What: Are the last 3 closing prices rising or falling?
Signal: Last close > close 3 bars ago (by >0.02%) = BULL. Last close < close 3 bars ago = BEAR. Otherwise NEUTRAL.
Why it matters: All other indicators are LAGGING — they use historical averages. This one is IMMEDIATE. It catches the actual price direction right now.
Also used as: Momentum confirmation gate (GATE 3). Even if 5 indicators say BULL, if the last 3 candles are falling, we skip the trade.

How Votes Are Aggregated

bull_pct = (bullish_votes / total_indicators) × 100 bear_pct = 100 - bull_pct Direction: bull_pct ≥ 60% → BULLISH (at least 3.6 out of 6 indicators) bear_pct ≥ 55% → BEARISH (lower bar because bear signals lag more) otherwise → NEUTRAL (no trade) NEUTRAL votes (RSI 45-55, price action flat) count as 0.5 bullish. Exhaustion filter: If bull_pct > 92% or bear_pct > 92%, SKIP. When ALL indicators agree, the trend is likely ENDING, not beginning.

Why Asymmetric Thresholds (60% bull vs 55% bear)?

Bearish signals are inherently harder to generate from lagging indicators. By the time EMA, Supertrend, and MACD all flip bearish, the RSI is already at 15-25 (extremely oversold) and the downtrend is exhausting. Lowering the bear threshold to 55% lets us catch bearish trends while there's still room to profit.

7. Exit Logic — 4-Stage Trailing SL + Partial Profits

Exit Priority (checked every tick)

PriorityConditionAction
1 (highest)Price ≤ Trailing SLImmediate market exit
2EOD 3:15 PMSquare off all MIS positions
350% of target reached + qty > 1 lotExit 50% quantity, trail SL to breakeven
4Price ≥ Target + held ≥ 2 candlesFull exit at target
5Trailing SL stages (see below)Tighten SL progressively
6Held > 2 hours with < 10% profitExit (theta is eating premium)

4-Stage Trailing Stop Loss

total_move = Target - Entry current_move = Current Price - Entry move_pct = (current_move / total_move) × 100 Stage 1: move_pct ≥ 30% (NIFTY) / 20% (BANKNIFTY) → Trail SL to BREAKEVEN (Entry price) → Trade is now risk-free Stage 2: move_pct ≥ 50% → Trail SL to Entry + 30% of current_move → Lock in 30% of unrealized profit Stage 3: move_pct ≥ 70% → Trail SL to Entry + 50% of current_move → Lock in 50% of unrealized profit Stage 4: move_pct ≥ 85% → Trail SL to Entry + 70% of current_move → Lock in 70% — let the last 30% run to target Example (NIFTY CE, Entry=200, SL=170, Target=260): total_move = 60 Price hits 218 (30%): SL trails from 170 → 200 (breakeven) Price hits 230 (50%): SL trails to 200 + 30×0.3 = 209 Price hits 242 (70%): SL trails to 200 + 42×0.5 = 221 Price hits 251 (85%): SL trails to 200 + 51×0.7 = 235.7

Partial Profit Booking

When price reaches 50% of target move AND quantity > 1 lot: Exit 50% of position at market Move SL to breakeven on remaining Let remaining 50% ride to full target Why: Data showed winners only captured 68% of max possible profit. Partial exit locks guaranteed profit while keeping upside.
